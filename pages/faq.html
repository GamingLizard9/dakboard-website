Frequently Asked Questions


How are you able to do this?

The company that makes DAKboards allows you to load their software onto other devices so people could assemble their own DAKboards.

Because this is a long process to set up, I have decided to offer it as a service.

Why shouldn't I buy from DAKboard?

In short: It's significantly more expensive, and you have to do the work yourself.

A DAKboard from them costs almost double what I charge for parts and installation ($550 vs. $300).

You can buy just the computer board from them, but that costs over $100, and you still need to purchase a monitor!

Finally, their microSD card costs $25, which I can reproduce for half that.

So, buying from me is cheaper from buying from them, and I do the installation!

How does making a purchase work?

First, reach out to me. Then, I will send you some products to buy on Amazon, which you will have shipped to your house.

Then, we arrange a time where I come to you and set it up.

Because you bought the parts yourself, you only pay me for labor.

Is this a touchscreen?

No. This display is meant to be controlled from your phone or computer, and it will automatically update from the apps on your device.

There is some functionality that can be done from a touchscreen, such as rotate the photo or scroll the calendar. However, I wouldn't reccomend this for a couple of reasons:

  1. The amount you can do with a touchscreen is very limited at this time.
  2. Touchscreen monitors are more expensive, so it will add at least $100 to the overall cost.
  3. Touchscreens don't work great with scheduling the board on/off, so if you have it turn off at night, it breaks the touchscreen.

Because of this, I wouldn't reccomend getting a touchscreen, but if you do, I can set it up.

How much does it cost?

The parts on Amazon flucuate in price, but are generaly betwee $150-$200.

How much is charged for labor depends on how long it takes me to set it up.

You can expect to pay $100 for setup if it takes less than 1.5 hours, and $50 for each additional hour.

The $300 figure on the front page is an estimate which is subject to change.

Why do some features require a subscription?

I am not the company who makes the software.

The company who makes the software charges a subscription fee for some features in order to cover the costs of servers to keep the software running.

If I had control over this, there would not be a subscription, but it is what it is.

Do not let this dissuade you from making a purchase; most features can still be accessed without one.

What do I get with a subscription?

If you pay their subscription, the main feature is custom layouts.

These allow you to choose exactly what appears on your screen, and exactly where it is.

You can do some customizing on the free plan, but paying for a subscription is a lot less limiting.

It also allows you to load 5 calendars instead of 2, and access to additional integrations.

You can compare the plans here.

How much does a subscription cost?

The "Essentials" plan costs $6 a month ($5 if billed yearly).

The "Plus" plan costs $10 a month ($8 if billed yearly). I would not reccomend purchasing this plan.

What calendar apps are supported?

DAKboard supports Google Calendar and Microsoft Calendar directly. This means you can simply log into your account and add the calendar without any additional trouble.

They also support any calendar that supports .ics links (which is almost all of them!)

What photo apps are supported?

The DAKboard software allows you to load background photos from the following sources:

  • iCloud Photos
  • Flickr
  • Google Photos
  • Google Drive
  • Dropbox
  • OneDrive
  • Image URL

You can also have it load the Bing daily image, or a random landscape picture.

What to-do apps are supported?

Currently, DAKboard supports Todoist, Google Tasks, and Microsoft To-Do.

DAkboard also has their own to-do list function which requires a subscription.

Could I use different parts if I wanted to?

Certainly! If you want to buy more or less expensive parts, go ahead!

If you would like to buy a different monitor, make sure it is IPS. Otherwise, it will not look good at an angle!

If you are buying a more expensive monitor, look into a 1440p or 4k monitor, or maybe one of a larger size!

However, buying a more powerful Raspberry Pi computer board will make little to no difference. I would not reccomend buying a cheaper one.

Remember, if you wanted to, you could always replace just the monitor later!
